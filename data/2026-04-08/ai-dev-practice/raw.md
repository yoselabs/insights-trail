**Key findings:**
- Over 70% of errors in modern LLM apps stem from incomplete/irrelevant/poorly structured context, NOT from model capability
- Context Engineering = the wider discipline designing and managing entire LLM input; RAG = a complementary technique within it
- The critical bottleneck in 2026 has shifted from "model side" to "context side"
- LLMs exhibit uneven attention in ultra-long contexts — beginning and end get disproportionate attention; critical info buried in the middle can be missed
- Chunking is the most underestimated lever for RAG performance
- Best practice: hybrid retrieval (semantic + BM25 lexical) + reranking for quality-critical applications
- Anthropic Contextual Retrieval: 49% reduction in failed retrievals; 67% with reranking added
- For knowledge bases under ~200K tokens: full-context prompting + prompt caching can be faster/cheaper than RAG infrastructure
- Index refresh cadence now standard: daily for dynamic content (product catalogs, compliance); hourly for real-time (customer support, news)
- "RAG-first, fine-tune-second" default recommendation for knowledge applications
