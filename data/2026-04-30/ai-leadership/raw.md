### Source 3: Optimum Partners — Engineering Management 2026: Structuring an AI-Native Team
**URL:** https://optimumpartners.com/insight/engineering-management-2026-how-to-structure-an-ai-native-team/

- "Context-First" completion standards required

### Source 6: Fortune — Meta's AI Team 50:1 Management Structure

### Source 7: Stanford Digital Economy Lab — Enterprise AI Playbook: 51 Successful Deployments
**URL:** https://digitaleconomy.stanford.edu/publication/enterprise-ai-playbook/
**PDF:** https://digitaleconomy.stanford.edu/app/uploads/2026/03/EnterpriseAIPlaybook_PereiraGraylinBrynjolfsson.pdf
**Authors:** Elisa Pereira, Alvin Wang Graylin, Erik Brynjolfsson
**Key Content:**
- Study of 51 successful enterprise AI deployments (published March 2026)
- 95% of AI transformation failures trace to organizational factors, not technology
- 77% of AI implementation challenges are not technical
- Four success factors: (1) workflow mapping before technology selection; (2) governance architecture embedded from day one; (3) observability established before production launch; (4) leadership continuity for first 18 months including through failed pilots
- Agentic implementations showed 71% median productivity gains vs 40% for high-automation
- Agentic implementations represented only 20% of cases
- In 42% of cases, model choice was interchangeable
- 61% of successful AI deployments followed at least one failed attempt
- "AI success is not about deploying better models. It is about redesigning how organizations work."
